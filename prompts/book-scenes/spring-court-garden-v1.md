# Spring Court Garden - Scene Prompt v1

## Book Reference
**A Court of Thorns and Roses** by Sarah J. Maas - The Spring Court, Tamlin's Manor

## Book Descriptions (for accuracy)
- Manor: "sprawling estate veiled in roses and ivy" with "alabaster sides"
- "patios and balconies and staircases sprouting from its alabaster sides"
- "giant glass doors leading to a second garden grander than the one out front"
- Flowers: "amethyst irises, pale snowdrops, and butter-yellow daffodils bloom in the balmy breeze"
- "rose garden planted as a mating gift from Tamlin's father to his mother"
- Landscape: "rolling green hills, lush forests, and clear, bottomless lakes"
- Fan interpretation: Art nouveau style, "trees growing into walls, flower and vine-like chandeliers"

## Requirements
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 (1920x1080 or higher)
- Key elements:
  - Bright, warm spring lighting
  - Roses prominently featured (book-accurate)
  - Alabaster/white stone architecture
  - Purple, yellow, white flowers as accents
  - Ivy on walls
  - Magical but grounded aesthetic
  - Clear sky or soft clouds

## Effect Pairing
- **Primary effect**: Floating flower petals / pollen
- **Config**: soft pink and white petals, slow drift, gentle swirl
- **Optional**: Subtle magical sparkles

## Scene Context
This is the garden courtyard of Tamlin's manor in the Spring Court - where Feyre spends much of the first book. It should feel romantic, magical, and full of life - the essence of eternal spring.
